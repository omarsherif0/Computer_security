About AES

AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) is a symmetric block cipher standardized by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) in 2001. It was chosen by the U.S. government to protect classified information and has become a global standard for data encryption. Common uses are: Protecting network communications (e.g., HTTPS, VPNs), Encrypting sensitive data in databases, and Securing IoT devices and systems.
